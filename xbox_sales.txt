Xbox hardware sales have experienced a significant and sustained decline throughout 2024 and 2025, extending into early 2026.
Key Financial Data

* Hardware Revenue Collapse: In the second quarter of fiscal year 2026 (Q2 FY26), Xbox hardware revenue plummeted by 32% year-over-year.
* Sustained Downtrend: This marks the eighth consecutive quarter of hardware decline, following previous drops of 29% in Q1 FY26 and 42% in Q4 FY24.
* Market Specifics:
* United States: Sales in November 2025 were reportedly down by 70% year-over-year, marking one of the worst months for the industry since 1995.
   * United Kingdom: 2025 was cited as the "worst year on record" for Xbox consoles, with a 39% annual sales drop. [1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8] 

Primary Drivers of the Decline

* Price Increases: Microsoft raised console prices multiple times in 2025, including hikes in May and October, which significantly suppressed consumer demand.
* Strategic Pivot: The company has shifted its focus away from hardware toward a "platform-agnostic" ecosystem, prioritizing Xbox Game Pass, cloud gaming, and PC.
* Multi-platform Releases: Bringing former exclusives to competing platforms (like PlayStation) has reduced the necessity of owning an Xbox console.
* Competitive Pressure: While Xbox sales cratered, competitors like the PlayStation 5 and the newly launched Nintendo Switch 2 maintained stronger market positions. [5, 9, 10, 11, 12, 13, 14] 

Current Outlook
Despite the hardware slump, Microsoft's overall gaming revenue is heavily supported by its acquisition of Activision Blizzard, though even content and services revenue saw a slight 5% dip in the most recent quarter. [15, 16]
